## 11. CS operational-band path — fixed
The old rule was `if (d0 <= 60) hold flat` — "anywhere under the band top, do nothing." Two failures:
- **South Korea** (54.4%) held flat at 54% forever while current policy fell to 28%, so the CS line looked *worse* than doing nothing.
- **Turkey** (24.7%) held at 24.7% — **below the 30% band floor** — while the readout still claimed "operational band reached".

New rule keys off the **45% central path**, not the band top:
- `d0 > 45` → retire down to 45% over the 26-year transition (shape-preserving anchors). Korea now 54.4 → **45%**.
- `d0 <= 45` → hold flat. The framework would never *issue* debt just to climb up to the band. Switzerland (36.1), Norway (41.1), Turkey (24.7) hold.

"Operational band reached" now prints **"at enactment"** rather than the literal start year when the country begins inside the band.

## 15. Retirement baselines localised — 11 of 15
**The sourcing pass produced a hard finding: outside the US and Australia, no country publishes a median private pension pot at retirement.** This is structural, not a search failure:
- Switzerland never centrally collects individual 2nd-pillar balances (they're tax-exempt while accumulating).
- Sweden: *"Det finns ingen myndighet som har statistikansvar för tjänstepensioner"* — and much of today's 55–65 cohort holds **defined-benefit** rights with no capital value at all. A Swedish "pot" isn't merely unmeasured; for many people it does not exist as a number.
- Norway, Japan, Korea publish means only.
- The ECB's euro-area survey measures only *voluntary* pensions — missing the pillar where the money actually sits.

## 17. CS line below the band — was reading as a bug, was actually under-stated
Turkey's CS line sat *below* the 30–60% shading with no explanation. Checked the papers rather than guessing, and they are explicit:

> "The 30 percent figure marks the lower edge of the band, **not a hard stop**; within the band, KT routes the growth-matched seigniorage **to citizen Stable Floors by default**, and to redemption only as needed to hold the band." — Statutory §4, KT definition

> "KT retires debt at full rate until the stock enters the band, then throttles to hold position, **so that the growth-matched seigniorage no longer needed for retirement is routed to citizen Stable Floors**." — Transition §4.1.1

So a low-debt country doesn't merely get a head start — **its citizens receive the issuance that would otherwise have gone to bondholders.** The chart annotation and the note now say this, and both passages are quoted in the sources block.

### ⚠ OPEN MODELLING GAP (needs a decision)
The engine does **not** implement that routing. The debt simulator and the Stable Floor engine are decoupled: KT is not one of the engine's channels, so for a country already inside the band, the ~1.5% of M2/yr that the papers say should flow to citizen Stable Floors is **not** added to the floor figures. Countries like Korea, Switzerland, Norway and Turkey are therefore **understated** on the Stable Floor / income tabs. Wiring it in would change headline floor numbers, so it was not done unilaterally.

## 19. Türkiye — no longer projected; shown as published
Sourcing pass (HMB / Hazine ve Maliye Bakanlığı, SBB Medium Term Program) found the old figure was wrong in **kind**, not just in value:

- **24.7% was stale.** It was the MTP's 2026 *projection* — and, coincidentally, the *old* 2024 actual before a GDP revision. Actuals: **23.8% (end-2025)**, **23.1% (2026 Q1)**. Baseline corrected to **23.1%**.
- **The projection was wrong in direction.** My model had Türkiye falling to 7% by 2100. Türkiye's own MTP projects the ratio **flat at ~24%** (24.7 → 24.7 → 24.2 for 2026–28).
- **Why:** the fall from 38.9% (2021) to 23.8% (2025) is *entirely* a denominator effect. The nominal stock rose **5.2×** over that window and Türkiye ran a deficit every year. Inflation inflated GDP faster than the debt. That mechanically stops as disinflation proceeds.
- **And it only ever eroded half the stock: 52.9% of central-government debt is FX-denominated**, and is revalued *upward* by lira depreciation, partly offsetting the erosion.

**So Türkiye is no longer modelled at all.** New generic mechanism: a country row may carry `history` (actuals) and `official` (its own published path) + `officialEnd`. When present:
- The red line is the **published MTP path**, held flat past 2028 — with an on-chart dashed marker reading *"official projection ends 2028 → held flat"*.
- A **history sparkline** renders above the chart showing the HMB actual series **2010–2025**, peak and latest labelled. This is the "something real to show in its place."
- The model chips (nominal growth, r − g) are **replaced** — showing them would imply a model is driving the line. They become: gross debt (2026 Q1 actual) · interest % of GDP · path provenance · "beyond 2028: held flat — not projected".
- The 2100 stat reads "held at the 2028 official endpoint" instead of a fabricated number.

The rate-convergence and uncertainty-band machinery was removed for Türkiye (it now has no projection to band). Japan keeps its 61–205% repricing band.
