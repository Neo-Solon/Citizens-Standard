What you're looking at

The Citizens Standard is a constitutional monetary framework that replaces central-bank discretion with three rules-based issuance channels. Every dollar of new money is distributed equally to all citizens — split between locked retirement equity (the Stable Floor) and, in inflation Mode, monthly dividends.

How to use: Pick a Mode below to see one of the framework's three constitutional configurations. Adjust any slider to build your own. The stat cards and charts update live. The vs. real outcomes tab compares your configuration to what Americans actually retire with under today's system.

Real-world data sources

401(k) balances: Vanguard, How America Saves 2025. Median balance at age 55-64 is $87,571; average is $244,750. Median is more representative of typical outcomes; the average is skewed by high-balance accounts.

Social Security: SSA Monthly Statistical Snapshot, March 2026. Average retired-worker benefit: $2,079.49/month ($24,953/year). Replacement rate for the average earner: ~37-42% of late-career earnings (CBO, SSA 2024-2025).

Trust fund depletion: 2025 SSA Trustees Report projects the OASI trust fund will be unable to pay full benefits beginning around 2034, after which only ~77-81% of scheduled benefits would be payable.

Alaska Permanent Fund Dividend: Alaska Department of Revenue. Recent dividends ranged $1,200-$3,300/year; default in this engine is $1,700.

Bitcoin: Treated as a savings vehicle at the user-set equity return for fair comparison. Real Bitcoin returns have been wildly volatile and are not predictive.

Engine methodology

Single-cohort projection: tracks one citizen born at year 0. K1 deposited once at birth; K2 deposited annually; K3 (when active) distributed monthly. Stable Floor compounds at the equity return rate. Real values deflate by CPI index. Each year, M2 expands by total issuance, GDP grows at real growth × CPI, and population grows at the pop-growth rate.

For comparison columns, "median outcome" uses real-world median 401(k) balance ($87,571) at retirement age and actual SS average benefit. "Average outcome" uses Vanguard's average 401(k) balance ($244,750) and average SS. "After SS trust depletion" applies a 23% cut to Social Security benefits per Trustees' projections. None of these scenarios assume perfect savings discipline over 43 years — they reflect what Americans actually retire with.

Honest caveats

K3 modeled as a flat percent of M2, not the paper's full price-level path targeting formula. Equity return treated as nominal — for real returns, set CPI to 0. Births computed as population × pop-growth (real demographics are more complex). The full paper's projections include stochastic returns, mode transitions, sequence-of-returns risk, and emergency tools — this engine is for intuition, not policy calibration.
