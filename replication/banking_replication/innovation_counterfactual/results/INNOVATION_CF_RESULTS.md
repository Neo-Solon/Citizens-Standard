# Innovation Counterfactual — Results

```
=== Bounded innovation counterfactual: %Δ business R&D under CS full reserve ===

  Grounded central (1% debt share, elast -2, +20% cost)
     ΔR&D = -0.40%

  Adversarial (5% debt share, elast -4, +30% cost)
     ΔR&D = -6.00%

  Extreme 'even if' (10% debt share, elast -4, +50% cost)
     ΔR&D = -20.00%

  Least responsive (1% debt share, elast -1, +20% cost)
     ΔR&D = -0.20%

READING
-------
The measured debt reliance of R&D-intensive firms is ~0.5% of assets [2],
and debt is NEGATIVELY correlated with R&D intensity [3]; external equity,
not debt, funds most of the rise in US R&D [4]. So the channel CS removes
is close to irrelevant for R&D finance. Even a deliberately adversarial 5%
debt share with the most-responsive elasticity gives only a -6% R&D change,
and an implausible 10%/-4/+50% 'even if' still only reaches -20%.

The grounded central estimate is about -0.4% -- economically negligible.

NOT MODELLED (both directions):
 + Favourable: CS routes issuance into a broad equity pool; since R&D is
   equity/own-funds financed [4], a deeper equity base plausibly offsets
   even this tiny loss. Unquantified.
 - Open: general-equilibrium demand channel (less credit-fuelled demand ->
   fewer profitable products). Needs behavioural assumptions; left open.

CONCLUSION: the DIRECT financing-cost channel of the innovation worry is
bounded and negligible. The residual uncertainty is the GE demand channel,
not the financing channel.
```
